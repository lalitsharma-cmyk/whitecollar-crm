# White Collar Realty CRM — Manager Presentation Outline

> Audience: Team Managers. Tone: practical, hands-on, coaching-focused.
> Each slide = title + bullets + a speaker note. ~8 slides.

---

## Slide 1 — Manager role in CRM

- You sit between Admin (Lalit) and your agents — with **team-wide visibility**, not just your own leads.
- See every lead, call, and deal for your team; reassign work; push stuck deals.
- Same login on phone and desktop — manage the floor from anywhere.
- You can do almost everything an agent can, **plus** monitoring, reassignment, and bulk import.
- What stays Admin-only: managing users/roles, setting Acefone IDs, and CSV exports.

> Speaker note: Position the manager as the "floor captain" — closest to the agents, responsible for daily momentum.

---

## Slide 2 — Team lead visibility

- **Leads** page shows your whole team, not just yours — filter by owner, status, team, AI score, or source.
- Each lead detail now shows: **Investor / End-user / Both** dropdown, a BANT card with **"N/4 captured" pill**, and a "why this score" explanation next to the AI score — so you can coach the quality of qualification, not just the quantity of calls.
- One-tap **smart filters**: Hot today, Ghosting, Site-visit potential, High budget, "Not picked 3+/7+ days".
- Follow-up windows built in: Today, Tomorrow, This week, This month, Overdue — each with a live count.
- **Team & Roles** page: each agent's active leads, workload, 90-day pipeline value, and average response time.
- Spot the over-loaded agent and the idle one at a glance via colour-coded workload.

> Speaker note: The workload + response-time columns are your early-warning system for who needs help today. The BANT "N/4 captured" pill is your coaching signal — if an agent's leads all show 1/4 or 2/4, that's the conversation to have.

---

## Slide 3 — Follow-up tracking

- Every lead carries a **follow-up date** — the heart of the system; no date means it gets forgotten.
- The **Action List** shows team priorities in three buckets: Ready to Close, Needs Your Attention, Overdue.
- Overdue follow-ups are flagged red and counted on the dashboard — chase these first.
- "Not picked" tags (3+/7+ days) make it easy to spot clients going quiet.
- Check who's clearing follow-ups vs letting them pile up — that's your daily coaching signal.

> Speaker note: Drill the golden rule into the team: every call ends with the next follow-up date set. Your job is to enforce it.

---

## Slide 4 — Pipeline movement

- **Pipeline Kanban**: New → Contacted → Qualified → Site Visit → Negotiation → Booking Done.
- At-risk and stuck cards auto-float to the top of each column — "Stuck X days", "No activity since stage change", "HOT going cold".
- Filter the board to one agent to review their whole pipeline in 30 seconds.
- Every stage change is logged with name + time, so progress is always honest.
- Watch the **closable deals** tile (Negotiation + EOI) — these are this month's bookings.

> Speaker note: Use the board in your daily stand-up — open it, scan the red flags, assign who pushes what.

---

## Slide 5 — Escalation process

- Agents tap **🆘 Escalate** on an Action List card when a deal needs a manager push.
- Escalated/flagged leads appear under **"Need Your Attention"** with the reason shown.
- The dashboard's **"Needs You"** KPI and the By-Salesperson "Needs Lalit" column keep these visible.
- You can **reassign** a lead to a stronger closer or a less-loaded agent in two taps.
- For untagged incoming leads, the **Awaiting Team** inbox lets you route them to Dubai or India.

> Speaker note: Make escalation feel safe, not like failure — "flag it early, we close it together."

---

## Slide 6 — Reports for manager

- **Reports hub** opens with the three questions that matter: revenue coming, biggest funnel leak, stalled-deal money.
- All reports share a **date-range picker** — set the window once and every chart updates. Back buttons mean you never lose your place.
- **Daily Report**: target vs achieved vs pending, per agent, per day.
- **Team comparison** (Dubai vs India), **SLA & Meetings**, **Cooling leads**, and **Lead Sources** are all one tap away.
- **Best-time-to-call heatmap** (IST, last 30 days) tells the team when pickups actually happen — use it to plan the day's calling window.
- Use these in weekly reviews — numbers, not opinions, drive the conversation.

> Speaker note: Cooling leads and the funnel-leak card are gold for coaching — they show exactly where deals slip. The shared date picker lets you pull a clean weekly or monthly window in seconds.

---

## Slide 7 — Coaching agents

- Use the **call records heatmap** + the **best-time-to-call chart** to coach *behaviour*, not just scold outcomes.
- Praise specific habits: detailed BANT remarks, follow-up streaks, fast first-call response.
- Spot patterns — an agent whose leads all show 1/4 BANT captured needs a different fix than one who just isn't dialling enough.
- **Call recordings with auto-transcription** mean you can review any call with the agent — not from memory, from the actual recording.
- The **Leaderboards** and gamified missions/XP make good habits visible and a bit competitive.
- Review each agent's **Action List** with them — it's the same view they see, filtered to their leads.

> Speaker note: The message: the CRM gives you evidence, so coaching becomes "here's what I see, let's fix this one thing." Note: the "Agent productivity" chart (call counts vs all-time leads) was removed because the numbers weren't comparable — use the Daily Report per-agent rows instead.

---

## Slide 8 — Daily manager checklist

- **Morning**: clear Awaiting Team, scan the Command Center hero tiles for your team.
- Check **overdue follow-ups** and the **By-Salesperson** table — who's behind, who needs a nudge.
- Work the **Action List** "Need Your Attention" bucket — push or reassign escalated deals.
- Open the **Pipeline** and clear the red-flag (stuck) cards with the owning agents.
- **End of day**: glance at the Daily Report; line up tomorrow's priorities and praise a win.

> Speaker note: Keep it to a repeatable rhythm — same five checks every morning. Consistency from the manager sets the floor's pace.
