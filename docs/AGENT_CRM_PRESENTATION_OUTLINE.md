# White Collar Realty CRM — Agent Presentation Outline

> Audience: Sales Agents (Mehak, Dinesh, Yasir, Tanuj). Tone: friendly, simple, motivational. No tech words.
> Each slide = title + bullets + a speaker note. ~11 slides.

---

## Slide 1 — Why CRM is being introduced

- One simple app for your whole day — works right on your phone.
- No more lost numbers, messy sheets, or "who was I supposed to call?"
- It remembers every client and every conversation **for** you.
- This is easier than the spreadsheets you're used to — promise.
- Built **for you**, to help you close more and stress less.

> Speaker note: Open warm. This isn't extra work or surveillance — it's a personal assistant that never forgets a lead.

---

## Slide 2 — How CRM helps agents

- Tells you **who to call next** — you don't have to decide.
- Keeps every client's story in one place: budget, what they want, last chat.
- Reminds you exactly when to follow up, so leads never go cold.
- Call and WhatsApp clients straight from their card — no copy-pasting numbers.
- Your effort gets counted — calls, wins, and streaks all show up.

> Speaker note: Frame every feature as "less typing, less remembering, more selling." That's the whole pitch.

---

## Slide 3 — Daily action list

- Your **to-do list** for the day — best client to call sits right on top.
- Work **top to bottom**; the order is already the smart plan.
- Three groups: 🔥 Ready to Close, 🚩 Need Your Attention, ⏰ Overdue.
- An empty Action List by end of day = a great day. 🎉
- Don't cherry-pick the easy ones — the top card is on top for a reason.

> Speaker note: This is the agent's home base. If they only learn one screen, make it this one.

---

## Slide 4 — Calling from CRM

- Tap **Call** on the lead — the system rings your phone, then dials the client (Acefone click-to-call).
- Read their history on screen *before* you talk — sound like you remember them.
- After the call, tap what happened: **Connected, Not picked, Callback, Interested, Not interested**, and more.
- Every call is saved automatically — if it isn't logged, it didn't happen.
- One tap, then move to the next card.

> Speaker note: Reassure them calling is easier, not harder — no dialling numbers by hand, and the log fills itself.

---

## Slide 5 — WhatsApp from CRM

- Tap the green **WhatsApp** button on a lead — the chat opens with their number already filled in.
- Send a brochure, a price, or a quick "great speaking with you."
- A friendly first-message draft is ready for you to send.
- Drop a quick remark after, so calls and messages live together in one place.
- On your phone it opens the WhatsApp app you already use. *(Side panel works once enabled.)*

> Speaker note: They already live on WhatsApp — the win is no more switching apps or hunting for numbers.

---

## Slide 6 — Adding remarks

- After every call, write a short, **real** note of what happened.
- Capture the full picture: *budget, what they want, when, who decides.*
- Example: "Budget 2.5 Cr, 3BHK Dubai Marina, ready in 3 months, wife decides."
- 🎤 **Speak instead of type** — tap the mic and talk in Hindi or English.
- Avoid empty notes like "will call later" — they help no one, including future-you.

> Speaker note: This is the habit that separates top performers. Push "specific remarks" hard — and show off the voice mic.

---

## Slide 7 — Setting follow-ups

- **The golden rule**: every lead leaves with a date for the next call.
- No date = the lead gets forgotten and goes cold. Always set a date.
- For a callback or busy line, pick the exact time — it pops back on your list that day.
- Your follow-ups show up under Today, Tomorrow, This week — nothing slips.
- Do this every time and you'll never lose a deal to "I forgot to call back."

> Speaker note: Repeat it like a chant: specific remark + follow-up date, every single lead. That's the job.

---

## Slide 8 — Cold data calling

- The **Revival Engine** gathers old, quiet leads — they're not dead, just forgotten.
- Old leads are gold nobody else is digging for. 💰
- Read the old notes first, then call with a warm "following up on the Marina apartment."
- Felt a spark? Tap **Promote to Lead** and it joins your active list.
- A daily mission, hidden gems, and a weekly leaderboard make it fun — one revived lead can make your month.

> Speaker note: Position cold data as treasure hunting, not punishment. The leaderboard and streaks are the hook.

---

## Slide 9 — Pipeline basics

- The pipeline is the journey: **New → Contacted → Qualified → Site Visit → Negotiation → Booking Done.**
- As a client warms up, move them one step forward — like climbing a ladder.
- Tap the stage label on the lead and pick the new stage.
- Made a mistake? Just change it back — every change is saved, no harm done.
- Honest stages = your real progress shows, and your wins get counted.

> Speaker note: Keep it light — it's just "where is this client today?" Moving cards is satisfying, not scary.

---

## Slide 10 — How CRM helps performance

- See your own numbers: calls made, connected, leads moved, follow-ups due.
- Earn **XP**, hit daily **missions**, and build **streaks** for good habits.
- A morning greeting picks your single most important task — your "today's mission".
- The **Vault** is your private space to journal, log wins, and reset on a tough day.
- Small daily habits → more connects → more bookings → bigger month.

> Speaker note: Make it personal and aspirational — the CRM is on their side, cheering their progress, not grading them.

---

## Slide 11 — Do's and Don'ts

- ✅ **Do** log every call — connected or not.
- ✅ **Do** set a follow-up date on every lead, every time.
- ✅ **Do** keep remarks specific — budget, want, when, who decides (use the mic!).
- 🚫 **Don't** leave a lead with no next step or write "will call later".
- 🚫 **Don't** skip the easy calls to look busy, and **don't** forget to punch in each morning.

> Speaker note: End on energy. Two habits win the game: log every call, set every follow-up. Do that and you're already a top performer. 💪
