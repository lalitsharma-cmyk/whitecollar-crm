## SLIDE 4: READING THE PIPELINE
**Headline:** "A healthy pipeline looks like a funnel. A sick one looks like a wall."

**Healthy shape:** Many leads at NEW → fewer at CONTACTED → fewer at QUALIFIED → very few at NEGOTIATION/EOI (every stage smaller than the last)

**Sick shapes to watch for:**
- Wall (flat across all stages) → Agents are not moving leads forward
- Bulge at CONTACTED → Qualification is failing — agents are talking but not confirming BANT
- Empty NEGOTIATION/EOI → No deals close to conversion — look at SITE VISIT completion rate
- Many "at risk" reds → Leads going cold; need immediate outreach

**Key action per shape:** [table with shape → action → URL to check]

**Notes for presenter:** Show the real /pipeline page. Point to the "at risk" badges. Show how to filter by Owner.

## SLIDE 5: COACHING WITH DATA
**Headline:** "Use specific numbers, not feelings."

**Three coaching scenarios:**

**Scenario 1 — Low call volume**
Data source: /reports/leaderboard → calls made column
Question: "Your call count this week is 8. The team average is 25. Tell me about your morning routine."

**Scenario 2 — High call count, low conversions**
Data source: Leaderboard → connect rate vs. conversion rate
Question: "You are making 30 calls a week but only 3% are converting to QUALIFIED. Let's listen to your pitch together."

**Scenario 3 — Many overdue follow-ups**
Data source: Action List → filter by agent → count overdue
Question: "You have 12 leads that are 3+ days overdue. Are you setting follow-up dates after each call?"

**Key message:** "The data tells you WHAT is happening. Your job is to ask WHY."

## SLIDE 6: RESPONDING TO ESCALATIONS
**Headline:** "When an agent flags a lead, they need a decision, not a delay."

**Escalation flow:**
1. Agent taps "Escalate" or "Flag for Manager Review" on a lead
2. You receive a notification (bell icon) + lead appears in your Action List under "Needs Attention"
3. Open the lead, read the agent's note
4. Respond within same working day

**Common escalation types and appropriate responses:**

| Escalation type | Your action |
|-----------------|-------------|
| Client wants special price/discount | Review commission math, call client or approve offer |
| Client wants to speak to senior person | Join the next call with the agent |
| Agent unsure about qualification | Review BANT fields in the lead, guide agent |
| Lead is stuck — agent cannot reach client | Review history, decide: reassign or archive |

## SLIDE 7: WEEKLY REPORTING AND TEAM REVIEW
**Headline:** "The four reports you run every week."

**1. Leaderboard (/reports/leaderboard)**
- Who is leading on calls? On conversions? On deals closed?
- Recognize public wins. Have private conversations about underperformance.

**2. Funnel Report (/reports)**
- Stage conversion rates: What % of CONTACTED leads become QUALIFIED? What % of QUALIFIED become SITE VISIT?
- If conversion drops week over week, find the stage and investigate.

**3. SLA Report (/reports/sla)**
- Which leads had a follow-up date pass with no action?
- Repeated SLA violations by the same agent = performance conversation.

**4. Daily Activity Feed (/reports/activity)**
- Review randomly on 2–3 days per week
- Open 5 call log remarks and assess quality
- If remarks are thin, address in team huddle (do not single out individuals)
